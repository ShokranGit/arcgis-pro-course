## Key concepts

GIS (Geographic Information Systems) and GIScience (the broader study of spatial concepts, methods, and their implications) are closely related but not identical: GIS is the software and technical practice, while GISc is the underlying discipline that studies how we represent, analyze, and reason about space. Modern GIS grew out of decades of work in cartography, computer science, and spatial statistics, and ArcGIS Pro is one of the current tools built on that history.

GIS is used across many fields — urban planning, public health, environmental science, logistics, emergency management, and more — because so many real-world questions are ultimately spatial ("where," "how far," "what's nearby"). At the same time, every map is a set of choices: what to include, how to symbolize it, and what projection to use. Those choices can inform readers clearly or, intentionally or not, mislead them. Thinking critically about a map's sources, symbology, and framing is as important as knowing how to build one.
