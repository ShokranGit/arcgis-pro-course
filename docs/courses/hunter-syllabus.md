## Course Description

This course offers an introduction to Geographic Information Systems (GIS), focusing on how spatial data can be integrated and analyzed to visualize relationships, uncover patterns, and develop solutions to real-world problems. Students will study the history of GIS, key concepts, data acquisition, editing, modeling, interpretation, analysis, and presentation. Emphasis will be placed on the effective use of spatial information in analysis and interpretation. The course includes a lab component that provides students with access to computers equipped with ESRI’s ArcGIS Pro Desktop software for hands-on practice. Lectures, tutorials, outside readings, discussions, class assignments, and practical exercises will guide students through the complete GIS workflow, from data input and management to spatial analysis and cartographic presentation. By integrating conceptual learning with applied work, students will develop the technical and analytical skills needed to design spatial projects, interpret results critically, and communicate findings effectively.

## Student Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course, students will be able to:

- Explain foundational concepts of Geographic Information Systems (GIS), spatial thinking, and geospatial data structures.
- Acquire, prepare, and manage spatial data from various sources, including vector, raster, GPS, and tabular datasets.
- Understand coordinate systems, map projections, and georeferencing, and apply them appropriately in GIS projects.
- Navigate and operate GIS software (e.g., ArcGIS Pro and QGIS), including data editing, mapping, spatial queries, and joins.
- Conduct spatial analyses such as buffering, overlay, proximity, and raster/vector operations to interpret spatial patterns and relationships.
- Create effective map layouts and visualizations that communicate data clearly using cartographic principles.
- Organize and maintain spatial databases, manage attributes and metadata, and apply good data management practices.
